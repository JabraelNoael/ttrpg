The 8 building blocks everything reduces to

Stat — any number: health, mana, luck… or invented ones (fear, footnote, debt, campfire_heat). Supports base×mult+flat + derived formulas.
Cost — an ability spends stats/items on cast; blocks the cast if you can't pay.
Reaction — applies stat deltas and/or calls functions on a target (@s).
Cooldown — turns before an ability can be recast.
Effect — a timed buff/debuff that ticks over N turns; can fire procs/reactions on chosen turns.
Talent — a passive that fires when proc-flags match (AND within a clause, OR across clauses).
Flag — a named on/off signal; pulse (momentary) or enable/disable (sticky). The DM's switch for "is this true right now?"
Function — a saved sequence of any commands, run with @s bound. The escape hatch for multi-step or whole-group effects.

How to translate any new ability

  • Quantify itAny number an ability cares about → a stat, even abstract ones (fear, trust, debt, footnote stacks, accumulated heat).
  • "Pay X to cast"→ a Cost on the ability.
  • "Gain / lose / buff Y"→ a Reaction (instant) or an Effect (over time).
  • "Whenever Z happens"→ a Talent keyed to a flag you pulse when Z happens (death/turn already auto-pulse).
  • "Do several things / to a group"→ a Function.
  • "Is this condition on now?"(in combat, being watched, plot tension high) → a flag the DM toggles.
  • Perception / narrative / ownership / AI / valuation / memory→ not modeled; lives in the DM's head, optionally mirrored as a flag or a calendar/quest note.

The ceiling — what no amount of modeling fixes

These are the recurring walls behind every ⛔: no line-of-sight / "who is watching", no parsing of speech or indirect references, no narrative/anticlimax awareness, no event interception/redirection, no ability-copying or self-modifying abilities, no autonomous entities pursuing objectives, and no physics, ownership graph, memory objects, or market valuation. Also: time is in turns, not seconds — map "60 seconds" → turns when building.

Most of these abilities were drafted without the engine in view, so landing 2 fully-automatic and 24 partially-scaffolded out of 40 is a good outcome — the partials still let the engine carry the bookkeeping (the cost, the buff, the timer) while you keep the judgment calls.
